Medical examiner request

"I'm very sorry to hear that they have passed. Can I take some more information and let the GP know?"

We can be informed of new deaths by a relative, by the hospital Bereavement Office, or by the Out of Hours team.

It is vital to check if the death has been verified by a clinician. For deaths in hospital, a hospital clinician will do this. For deaths out of hospital, this can be done by paramedics, some district nurses, and some care home nurses.

If the death has not been verified, inform the duty GP that this needs to be done by booking URGENT RESPONSE section of acute triage.

Make a Daybook announcement regarding the death so that all clinicians are informed.

Inform the PCA assigned to Registrations so that the deceased patient can be inactivated promptly.

If an out of hospital death, proceed to death certificate request

"I will ask the GP to check they are in a position to issue the death certificate. They will send information to the Medical Examiner and then to the Registry Office. You do not need to collect the certificate."

"Can I take your details as the next of kin?"

A GP will be able to propose a cause of death if they have attended the deceased.

Find the GP that last had a consultation with the patient. Send an urgent Daybook task to them requesting death paperwork.

If this GP is not available e.g. on leave, find the next GP to have a consultation and task them instead.

If the patient was newly registered and had no consultations, book on URGENT ADMIN section of triage list.

The coroner's office will request information if they need to investigate to determine the cause of death.

They will initially ask for an 'encounter report' for the patient. This can be found in Guidelines.

Print, scan and send the relevant information as requested. We are legally obliged to comply with all requests for information from the coroner's office.

If the coroner's office needs a GP report, they will send a letter requesting this. Send a Daybook task to the last GP to see the patient, letting them know when the report is due.

The Medical Examiner's service inspect every death and co-sign the death certificate.

They will request access to the patient's notes to do this. We are legally obliged to comply with all requests for information from the Medical Examiner's service.

Send the relevant information as requested.

"We can only accept urine samples that have been requested. Could you please tell me who requested this and why?"

Urine samples may be requested for investigation of urinary symptoms from a PCA triage protocol or by a clinician.

Urine samples are also requested to check for protein in the urine (ACR) as part of diabetes and chronic kidney disease monitoring.

If they are unclear, check the patient's notes to determine the reason and whether a request form has been generated.

If still unclear, ask the duty GP to advise (book as URGENT ADMIN on acute triage list).

"Thanks for providing your blood pressure readings. Our healthcare assistant will review these and let you know the outcome."

"Is it ok if we send you a text message if they are normal?"

Home blood pressure monitoring has a role in diagnosing and monitoring Hypertension.

Patients may drop in their readings. These should be given to a healthcare assistant for them to action.

If the readings are very high, they will have contact from the duty GP. If they are above target, they will be contacted routinely by a GP. If they are normal they will be informed by the HCA.

Shared care is a system where we prescribe and monitor specialist medications on behalf of the hospital team.

We receive requests from the hospital to initiate shared care.

The request needs to be signed by a GP, scanned and returned. Book an ADMIN slot and put the paperwork in the GP's tray.

We cannot prescribe medications until the agreement is in place and the patient is stabilised. If a patient has a query regarding this, book a query slot (red) with practice pharmacist.

Monitoring is managed by the healthcare assistant team. Direct monitoring queries to them.
